Presentation 1: Preparation Session Instructions

Oral Presentation Assessment 1 — Research Story-telling by Experienced Writers (20%)

Overview

This document gives you everything you need to prepare for Oral Presentation 1. You will present an empirical journal article from your field for 8 minutes to a non-specialist audience. Your talk must cover four areas: (1) introduction, (2) key findings, (3) significance, and (4) impact on your own research design and writing lessons learned.

Important: Reading from a script is not permitted and will result in a failing grade. Note cards with key points are allowed.

What to Prepare in Advance (Before the Session)

You should have completed these steps before coming to the preparation session. Please update your forked version of the repository and post a brief update to the course forum.

Step 1: Fork and Clone the Repository

  1. Fork the course repository: https://github.com/tesolchina/mccpSpring2026
  2. Clone your fork to your local machine (see cursor_cloning_mccpspring2026_repositor.md in pre1/materials/ for a walkthrough)
  3. Navigate to pre1/teachingAssessment/pre1work/ — this is your workspace

Step 2: Select Your Paper

  • Choose an empirical journal article in your field
  • The paper should be in a machine-readable format (PDF, HTML, or text)
  • Place a copy in pre1work/papers/

Step 3: Generate Your Outline with AI

  1. Open pre1work/genOutline.md — this contains instructions for the AI agent
  2. The AI will read your paper + the assessment requirements and generate:
    • A presentation outline covering the 4 required sections
    • Presenter notes aligned with each rubric criterion
    • A plan for HTML slides
  3. Review the outline and write your feedback in pre1work/feedback.md
  4. The AI will revise and generate HTML slides (see genHTMLguide.md for design principles)
Tip: Study the demo in pre1workDemo/ to see a complete example — from paper to finished HTML slides — using the KVFlow paper.

Step 4: Practice Your Delivery

  • Practice out loud (not in your head) — at least 3 times
  • Time yourself — aim for exactly 8 minutes
  • Record yourself speaking and listen back for filler words, pacing issues
  • Focus on speaking from key points, not reading sentences

Step 5: Post to the Forum

  • Update your forked repository with your outline and slides
  • Post a brief reply to the course forum with:
    • Your paper title and why you chose it
    • A link to your forked repo (or the generated HTML slides if published)
    • One question or challenge you encountered during preparation

3-Hour Session Timeline

0:00 — 0:15 (15 min)
Welcome & Check-In
Quick overview of today's goals. Students confirm: (a) paper selected, (b) outline generated, (c) slides drafted. Instructor addresses common issues from forum posts.
0:15 — 0:45 (30 min)
Peer Review of Outlines & Slides
In pairs or groups of 3, share your outline and slides. Compare notes on: (a) Is the content accessible to non-specialists? (b) Are the 4 required sections clear? (c) Do the slides support (not duplicate) the speech? Give each other written feedback.
0:45 — 1:00 (15 min)
Delivery Tips Briefing
Instructor-led walkthrough of key delivery skills: body language, eye contact, voice modulation, and the 5-second mini-conversation technique. See Delivery Tips (interactive) and the deliveryTips.md reference guide.
1:00 — 1:10 (10 min)
Break
Take a break. Review your notes and mentally rehearse your opening.
1:10 — 2:30 (80 min)
3-Minute Delivery Practice (All Students)
Each student delivers the first 3 minutes of their presentation. This is NOT about content completeness — focus entirely on delivery:
  • Body language: Posture, stance, hand gestures
  • Eye contact: Looking at people, not slides or notes
  • Voice: Volume, pace, tone variation, pausing
  • Interaction: Speaking TO the audience, not AT them

After each student: 1-2 minutes of peer feedback using the criteria above. Instructor provides brief targeted comments.

With ~15 students: 3 min talk + 2 min feedback = 5 min per student = 75 min. Adjust as needed.

2:30 — 2:45 (15 min)
Reflection & Revision Planning
Based on feedback received, write down 3 specific things to improve. Update your slides or outline if needed. Commit changes to your forked repo.
2:45 — 3:00 (15 min)
Wrap-Up & Next Steps
Instructor reminds students of: (a) assessment date and time, (b) upload slides 2 hours before, (c) peer review responsibilities. Quick Q&A on any remaining concerns.

Assessment Rubric Summary

Criterion Focus Areas Weight
Content, Structure & Interaction Non-specialist audience, reflection integrated into analysis, logical flow, transitions, Q&A, peer evaluation /10
Delivery, Body Language & Timing Clarity, intonation/volume/stress, eye contact, posture, gestures, no script-reading, timing /10
Use of Visual Aids Slide design quality, slides supplement (not duplicate) the speech /10
Language Range & Accuracy Communicative effectiveness, grammatical accuracy, vocabulary range /10

Total: /40
