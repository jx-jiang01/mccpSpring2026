Input
A draft of the methodology section of my paper + reviewer comments pointing out that the experimental setup is unclear and lacks justification for parameter choices.
Process
Ask the AI to rewrite the methodology section to improve clarity and logical flow, explicitly explain the experimental setup, and add concise justifications for key parameter settings, while keeping the original technical content unchanged.
Output
A revised methodology section with clearer explanations of the experimental design and parameter choices, improved readability, and brief notes highlighting where and how the revisions address the reviewer’s concerns.

3. Thematic Analysis

  • Literature & discovery: Many students use AI for ideation, literature search, and summarising papers. Tools: ChatGPT, Doubao, Gemini, Consensus. Common workflow: input topic or PDFs → output outlines, comparisons, references.
  • Drafting & revision: Polishing, grammar checks (Grammarly, etc.), and paragraph-level revision are widespread. Several wish for clearer structure feedback (e.g. paragraph order, merge/split) and preservation of author voice.
  • Technical & code: Coding support (Cursor, Codex, DeepSeek), reproducibility checks, and BibTeX/format fixes appear often. Some wish for AI to verify code against papers or to maintain “consistent memory” across coding and writing.
  • Translation: Translation of papers or passages is frequently mentioned, especially for reading and drafting.
  • Trust & quality: Recurring wishes: reduce hallucination, better logical/mathematical consistency, technical-term tooltips (e.g. clickable definitions), and more natural revision so that AI-edited text is less detectable.
